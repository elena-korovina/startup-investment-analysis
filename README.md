# Startup Investment Market Analysis

## About the Project

This project was completed for a venture investment company interested in understanding startup funding patterns and identifying promising investment opportunities.

The analysis is based on historical data on startup financing and investment returns. The study explores funding activity, investment types, market segments, and return dynamics to support investment decision-making.

More than **50,000 startup companies** were analyzed.

---

## Business Objective

The goal of the project was to identify:

- promising industries for investment;
- the most popular funding types;
- trends in startup financing activity;
- characteristics of successful investment strategies;
- funding instruments associated with higher returns.

The results can be used to support investment decisions and portfolio planning.

## Dataset

The analysis is based on two datasets.

### `cb_investments.csv`

Contains information about:

- startup companies;
- market sectors;
- funding rounds;
- investment amounts;
- funding types;
- company status.

### `cb_returns.csv`

Contains annual return data for different funding types.

Overall scope:

- 50,000+ startups;
- dozens of market segments;
- multiple funding instruments and investment stages.

---

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Preprocessing

- data type validation;
- duplicate detection and removal;
- missing value analysis;
- date and numerical field validation.

### 2. Market Segmentation

- classification of market segments;
- identification of mass, medium-sized, and niche sectors;
- analysis of market structure.

### 3. Funding Analysis

- analysis of funding rounds;
- comparison of investment types;
- identification of typical and anomalous funding volumes;
- outlier detection and filtering.

### 4. Investment Dynamics

- analysis of funding trends over time;
- evaluation of changes in investment activity by year;
- assessment of growing market sectors.

### 5. Return Analysis

- comparison of returns across funding types;
- evaluation of investment efficiency;
- identification of funding instruments with the highest return potential.

---

## Key Findings

- More than 50,000 startup companies were analyzed.
- Venture funding and traditional investment rounds represented the largest share of investments.
- Several market sectors demonstrated sustained growth in funding activity.
- Investment volumes varied significantly across industries and funding stages.
- Differences in return rates suggest varying levels of investment risk and potential profitability.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the historical data available before 2015:

- focus on rapidly growing market segments;
- prioritize funding instruments with stable return patterns;
- consider diversification across industries and funding stages;
- evaluate both investment volume and return potential when building an investment portfolio.
